    FAQ Page

    Please see http://meta.census.okfn.org/ for more about Local Open Data Censuses

    • who is behind this website?
      this website was built by the coordination team of school of data, under the guidance of sheena carmel opulencia-calub, as part of her school of data fellowship. school of data is a network of data literacy practitioners, both organisations and individuals, implementing training and other data literacy activities in their respective countries and regions.
    • what is this website for?
      the goal of this website is to produce a public local information resource centered around water, sanitation and hygiene (wash) needs, to help with emergency preparedness and action-taking. it doubles as a comparison tool ranking the wash data quality and availability of the 225 cities and provinces of the philippines.
    • why focus on water hygiene and sanitation?
      assembling all indicators linked to disaster preparedness and integrating into the website would be a huge undertaking, beyond the scope of a single fellowship project. which is why the chosen indicators are focused on water, hygiene and sanitation, which happens to be the speciality of sheena carmel opulencia-calub. as a result, we have a website which gives accurate information on a small coherent set of indicators, which is enough to support evidence-based decision-taking among local governments in the philippines.
    • how is the preparedness score calculated?
      the preparedness score is calculated based on acceptable levels identified by international organizations such as world health organization (who) and by local government development goals and indicators. based on these acceptable levels, the datasets were categorized into three score levels: poor for very low levels, moderate for average levels, and high for acceptable levels.
    • where does the data come from?
      the data will come from national government database e.g. field health service information system (fhsis) of the department of health, office of civil defense (ocd), and sub-national government database.
